<h2>The Dukakis virus</h2>

The first virus for Apple Hypercard, and also I believe the first virus that promoted a political 
candidate, was the "Dukakis" virus. It was spread to Compuserve in 1988, but never got a lot of 
traction and did no damage. 

However, it showed that it was possible to use the Hypertalk language to make viral code, which was
exploited by later and more prolific viruses.

The author, the then 14-year old Steven Merel (name use with permission), says:

<em>"I remember suddenly realizing that it would be possible to write self-replicating code in HyperCard, 
and not being able to resist the urge to prove it. This was an earlier time, of course, when people’s 
lives were not online and computer viruses were not the mainstream threat they are now. I created a 
simple stack that just let the user play around with HyperCard’s built-in text-to-speech feature, just 
to have some vector for the virus that people might want to download, and uploaded it to CompuServe, 
using my 1200-baud modem.  I think it might have been called something like “MacVoice”.

A few days later I got a phone call from the moderator of whatever Mac user group I had uploaded it to. 
I actually recognized his name, as he was a regular columnist for MacWorld magazine (or maybe MacUser?) 
which I read religiously.  He asked if I knew I had uploaded a virus to CompuServe, and I pretended 
ignorance.  He asked how old I was, and I remember lying and telling him I was 13, since I knew the virus 
said I was 14.  Clearly he knew I was lying, and I was sure I was going to get in big trouble, though I 
never heard anything about it after that.

I’m particularly amused by the fact that I commented the code; clearly I wanted people to appreciate it!"</em>
