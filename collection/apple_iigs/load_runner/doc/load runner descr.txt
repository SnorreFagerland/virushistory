Lode Runner Virus Description
~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~
Lode Runner is an Apple II virus that destroys the data on any disk, virus-
infected or not.  The virus is triggered by a certain date reported by the
system clock.  When the system clock reports the month of October, an odd-
numbered day, and a minute value divisible by 8, the virus is ready to be 
triggered.  Triggering occurs when you boot the system with either an infected 
or non-infected disk.  Once triggering occurs, data on the boot disk are erased.

Lode Runner spreads itself to other disks by patching the "boot blocks" or
ProDOS kernel that is part of every ProDOS-formatted disk.  When you boot an
infected disk, part of the virus also installs itself in system memory, making
it a permanent part of the current session.

Unlike other viruses, there is no way to see this virus progressing under 
normal system use.  It has been reported that when the virus is present, the
Sierra OnLine game SPACE QUEST I will fail to run.  The virus cannot spread to
write-protected disks, nor does it infect 5-1/4" floppy disks.  However,
because of its memory-resident mechanism, it can spread to new disks even if 
a non-infected disk was last booted.
