** Arcuebus (NetStatus); Strains known: 2 (9616, 9616 + Module)
   Module that installs a module by the name of NetStatus (that natty thing
   that sets the LEDs on the hourglass for network trafic).  Eight possible
   file names.  Adds a load line to the !Boot file.
   
** AxisHack
   Never seen in the wild.
   
** BBCEconet; Strains known: 1 (5284)
   Parasite that attaches to Absolute files and spreads using a module named
   BBCEconet (a module to emulate the BBC's Econet device)
   
** BigFoot; Strains known: 2 (5535 5580)
   BASIC program that adds a load line to the !Boot file.
   
** Boohoo
   Never seen in the wild.
   
** BRB (aka Be Right Back) Strains known: 3
   Semi-polymorphic BASIC program with an assembler stub, which is random.
   The filename, length, type and encryption key is different after every
   infection.  Some strains also keep track of their infections, and reinfect
   when the infection is removed by Killer.
   
** Breakfast; Strains known: 1 (6688)
   Parasite that attaches to Absolute files.  Installs a module under the
   name of BBCEconet.

** Module; Strains known 2 (956 1112)
   Parasite that attaches to modules.  The 1112 strain can also bypass FSLock
   on machines with RISC OS 3.5 or higher.
   
** MonitorDAT; Strains known 2 (2168 2355)
   Vigay strain that activates on Mondays.  The 2168 strains causes the
   screen to 'disintegrate' as well as wobble, where 2355 just wobbles.
   Surprisingly, Rob Kendrick (one of Slayer's authors) was acused of
   writing this one.  What is it about this virus and AV people?  It's only
   a matter of time before there are strains with 'Alan Glover' or
   'Dr. Alan Soloman' at the top...
